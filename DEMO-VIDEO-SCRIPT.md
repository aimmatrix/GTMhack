# Demo Video — Nuudle × Lightfern

**Format:** ~70-second skit + closing cards for hackathon judges
**Concept:** A live-action skit showing cold pitch failure → platform insight → personalised pitch wins
**Example:** Selling coffee beans to a coffee shop owner
**Tone:** Funny, physical, real — the skit IS the value prop
**Tagline:** "From your first client to your next hundred."

---

## THE STORY

You're selling coffee beans. You walk into a coffee shop and pitch cold — rejected. You pull out Nuudle, discover the owner is a fitness nut who runs "Coffee & Calisthenics" events. You go back, connect through push-ups, and close the deal. The platform found the angle. You just had to show up differently.

---

## SCENE 1 — THE COLD PITCH (0:00–0:15)

**Location:** A real coffee shop (or mock one).

| Shot | Visual | Audio |
|---|---|---|
| A — Walk in | You enter. Suit energy, clipboard energy. Confident but generic. | "Hi, I sell premium coffee beans, can I show you our—" |
| B — Rejected | She cuts you off. Hand up. Not rude — just tired. | "No thanks. I get twenty of you a week. Bye." |
| C — Walk out | You leave. Door closes. Defeated. | — |
| Overlay | Text on screen: **"Cold outreach. 2% reply rate."** | — |

**Direction:** Fast. Don't linger. The rejection should feel familiar — every founder has lived this.

---

## SCENE 2 — THE PLATFORM (0:15–0:25)

**Location:** Outside the coffee shop, or a bench nearby.

| Shot | Visual | Audio |
|---|---|---|
| A — The screen | You open your laptop. Nuudle is on screen. You type the coffee shop name. The platform shows her profile with the key insight highlighted: **"Hosts Coffee & Calisthenics Saturdays. Values fitness-aligned brands. Active in local fitness community."** | No voiceover. Quiet. Let the audience read the screen. |
| B — The reaction | Close-up on your face. You see it. You smile. You close the laptop. | — |

**Direction:** This is only 10 seconds but it's doing ALL the heavy lifting. The screen must be readable on camera — big text, insight highlighted/circled. If judges miss the screen, the push-ups seem random. Consider a zoom-in on the insight text.

**Critical:** The platform screen must look like a real product, not a text editor. Coordinate with the frontend team — even a static screenshot of the UI with this data is fine.

---

## SCENE 3 — THE PERSONALISED PITCH (0:25–0:55)

**Location:** Back inside the coffee shop.

| Shot | Visual | Audio |
|---|---|---|
| A — Walk back in | Different energy. Casual. No clipboard. Warm. | "Hey — I saw you do Coffee & Calisthenics on Saturdays. I actually sponsor three fitness pop-ups in Hackney. And honestly—" |
| B — Drop | You drop to the floor and start doing push-ups. | — (just the sound of push-ups) |
| C — Her reaction | She's surprised, then laughing. She's impressed. | — |
| D — She joins | She starts doing push-ups too. She does more than you. You're both on the floor. Laughing. | — |
| E — Stand up | You both stand up. Catching your breath. The energy is completely different from Scene 1. | — |
| F — The close | You look at her. She's listening now. | "So — about those beans. Single-origin, perfect for a fitness crowd that cares about what they put in their body." |
| G — Handshake | She shakes your hand. Deal done. She's smiling. | "Let's talk." |
| Overlay | Text on screen: **"Personalised outreach. Powered by Nuudle × Lightfern."** | — |

**Direction:**
- The push-up moment is the peak. Let it breathe — don't rush it.
- SHE should do more push-ups than you. You lose, you both laugh. The sale comes from connection, not competition.
- The contrast between Scene 1 (stiff, generic, rejected) and Scene 3 (warm, personal, accepted) IS the entire pitch. Make it obvious.
- The handshake should feel earned, not forced.

## WHY THIS WORKS

| What judges see | What they understand |
|---|---|
| Generic pitch → instant rejection | Cold outreach is dead |
| Platform screen → specific insight | Nuudle finds non-obvious angles |
| Push-ups + laughing together | Personalisation builds real connection |
| Handshake → deal closed | Connection → conversion |
| "First client → next hundred" | This scales beyond one coffee shop |

The product is SHOWN (10 seconds of screen), not explained. The value prop is FELT (rejection → connection → deal), not narrated. Judges remember stories, not feature lists.

## PRODUCTION CHECKLIST

### Before filming
- [ ] Find a real coffee shop or convincing set (even a kitchen counter with a coffee machine works)
- [ ] Cast the coffee shop owner — she needs to be natural, able to do push-ups, and willing to improvise
- [ ] Prepare the Nuudle product screen — coordinate with frontend team for a real-looking UI screenshot with the insight data
- [ ] Practice the push-up sequence — it needs to look spontaneous, not rehearsed
- [ ] Prepare the closing branded cards (branding team)
- [ ] Decide: voiceover on the close, or text only?

### The platform screen (Scene 2)
- [ ] Text must be large enough to read on a laptop screen filmed from 2–3 feet away
- [ ] Key insight should be visually highlighted (bold, coloured, circled — whatever the UI supports)
- [ ] The screen should show: shop name, the "Coffee & Calisthenics" insight, and "Values fitness-aligned brands"
- [ ] Consider filming the screen separately and compositing — avoids glare and resolution issues

### The push-up moment (Scene 3)
- [ ] Film from a low angle during push-ups — more dynamic
- [ ] She must do more push-ups than you — this is important for the tone
- [ ] The laughter must be real — if it's forced, reshoot
- [ ] Allow 15–20 seconds for the push-up sequence — don't rush the comedy

### Audio
- [ ] Dialogue can be recorded live if the location is quiet enough
- [ ] Music: starts sparse (Scene 1), builds energy (Scene 3 push-ups), full and confident (Scene 4 close)
- [ ] Consider dropping music entirely during push-ups — just the sound of effort + laughter

### Closing cards
- [ ] Match the branding team's visual identity (colours, fonts, logo)
- [ ] Keep text minimal — one phrase per card, large type
- [ ] Landing page URL must be live by filming time

## FALLBACK PLAN

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Coffee shop location falls through | Film in any space with a counter + coffee machine. Even a kitchen. |
| Coffee shop owner can't do push-ups | She just watches and laughs. You do the push-ups solo. Still works. |
| Platform screen has glare on camera | Screen-record the UI separately, composite in post. |
| Product UI isn't ready | Use a clean mockup — static Figma screen with the insight data. Judges see it for 5 seconds. |
| Skit runs too long | Cut Scene 1 shorter (10s instead of 15s). The rejection can be faster. |
| Skit runs too short | Add a brief moment after the handshake — she hands you a coffee, you cheers. Warm ending. |
